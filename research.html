Research Paper

NepOOC-M: Bilingual Nepali-English Benchmark and Comparative Analysis of Multimodal Architectures for OOC Detection Preprint

First author

Out-of-context (OOC) misinformation pairs real, unaltered images with misleading captions to construct false narratives — making detection a problem of text-image alignment rather than image forensics. Despite the prevalence of this kind of misinformation in Nepal, no public benchmark previously existed for it.

This paper introduces NepOOC, the first publicly available Nepali-dominant, multilingual OOC benchmark, containing 1,090 image-caption pairs (545 pristine, 545 OOC) annotated across five misinformation typologies with strong inter-annotator agreement (Cohen's κ = 0.84). It then systematically evaluates five multimodal architectures against text-only and image-only baselines.

The central finding is that a text-only mBERT model performs statistically indistinguishably from the best multimodal system (ResNet-50 + mBERT), while image-only models perform near chance. This challenges the common assumption that multimodal fusion always helps in low-resource settings, and suggests that for Nepali OOC detection, dataset expansion is currently a more promising direction than architectural complexity.

Research Interests

  • Multimodal: How can models jointly reason over text, vision, and speech?
  • Multilingual: How can we make NLP systems work for all of the world's languages?
  • Transfer Learning: How can models use knowledge gained from a source task for a target task?
  • Explainable AI (XAI): How can we make AI systems more transparent and interpretable, especially in high-stakes and low-resource settings?

Outside NLP, I am also open to exploring research in related domains such as Social Network Analysis, Computer Vision, and Multimodal Biomedical Informatics.
